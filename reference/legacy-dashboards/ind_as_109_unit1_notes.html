1. Concept & Scope Last updated: Today, 10:30 AM

1. Financial Instrument

“
A financial instrument is:
"A contract that gives rise to a financial asset of one entity and a financial liability or equity instrument of another entity."
Key points:
  • There must be a contract.
  • Contract need not always be written.
  • Non-contractual obligations are not financial instruments.
  • Statutory obligations like income tax are not financial liabilities.
  • Constructive obligations under Ind AS 37 are not financial liabilities.
2. Asset Classification

2. Financial Asset

A financial asset includes:
  • cash
  • equity instrument of another entity
  • contractual right to receive cash or another financial asset
  • contractual right to exchange financial instruments under potentially favourable conditions
  • certain own-equity-settled contracts
Common examples:
  • cash
  • bank balance
  • trade receivables
  • loans receivable
  • deposits given
  • investment in bonds/debentures
  • investment in equity shares of another entity
  • perpetual debt instrument held by investor
Not financial assets:
  • inventory
  • PPE
  • right-of-use assets
  • intangible assets
  • prepaid expenses
  • advance for goods/services
Reason:
These may generate cash in future, but they do not create a present contractual right to receive cash or another financial asset.
3. Liability Classification

3. Financial Liability

A financial liability includes:
  • contractual obligation to deliver cash or another financial asset
  • contractual obligation to exchange financial instruments under potentially unfavourable conditions
  • obligation to settle using variable number of own equity shares
  • derivative over own shares that fails fixed-for-fixed
Common examples:
  • bank loan
  • trade payable
  • bills payable
  • deposits received
  • redeemable preference shares
  • debentures/bonds issued
  • financial guarantee given
Not financial liabilities:
  • advance from customer for goods/services
  • deferred revenue
  • most warranty obligations
  • statutory obligations like income tax payable
Reason:
Settlement is by goods/services/statute, not contractual delivery of cash or another financial asset.
4. Guarantee Contracts

4. Financial Guarantee

A financial guarantee is still financial-instrument territory even though payment is contingent.

“
Example Scenario:
Parent guarantees subsidiary’s bank loan.
Parent classification:
  • financial liability / financial guarantee contract
Reason:
Parent has a contractual obligation to pay cash if the subsidiary defaults.
5. Residual Interest

5. Equity Instrument

“
An equity instrument is:
"A contract that evidences residual interest in the assets of an entity after deducting liabilities."
Practical test: Can the issuer avoid paying cash?
• If yes: equity-like.
• If no: liability-like.
Examples of equity:
  • ordinary shares
  • non-puttable ordinary shares
  • irredeemable preference shares with discretionary dividend
  • CCPS converting into fixed number of shares, dividend discretionary
  • warrant to acquire fixed number of shares for fixed amount
6. Substance Rules

6. Preference Shares

“
Main rule:
"Legal form does not decide classification. Substance of contractual terms decides."
Liability Features:
  • Redeemable at fixed date = financial liability
  • Redeemable at holder’s option = financial liability
  • Mandatory dividend = liability feature
  • Cumulative dividend with no unconditional right to defer = liability feature
Equity Features:
  • Redeemable at issuer’s option = equity until issuer exercises option
  • Dividend discretionary = equity feature
  • Dividend payable only if declared = equity feature
Important accounting notes:
• Inability to pay due to lack of funds/profits/legal restrictions does not remove contractual obligation.
• Past history/intention/market expectation of dividend does not create liability if payment remains discretionary.

7. Settlement Rules

7. Own Equity Settlement

“
The rule:
"Fixed amount of cash for fixed number of own shares = equity."
Memory version:
• Fixed number of shares = owner risk = equity
• Variable number of shares worth fixed money = liability
• Failed fixed-for-fixed = financial asset/liability

Practical Scenarios:

Scenario Contract Settlement Type Ind AS Classification
Each CCPS converts into 1 equity share Fixed for fixed Equity
Warrant to buy 1,000 shares at Rs. 100 each Fixed cash for fixed shares Equity
Issue shares worth Rs. 10 lakh, number decided using future market price Variable shares worth fixed cash Financial Liability
8. Split Accounting

8. Compound Financial Instrument

“
Description:
Some instruments have both liability component and equity component. Detailed accounting comes in Unit 3; for Unit 1, just identify that mixed instruments may be compound.
Examples of compound instruments:
  • convertible bond
  • instrument with mandatory cash obligation plus equity conversion feature
  • certain preference shares with mixed features

12. Commodity Scope

12. Own-Use Exemption

“
Own-use physical delivery rules:
Contracts to buy/sell non-financial items are outside Ind AS 109 if: entered into for expected purchase/sale/usage, continue to be held for that purpose, physical delivery happens, and not net settled.

Exemption Flow Verification

Evaluate your contract criteria:
Entered into and continues to be held for expected physical purchase/usage.
No historic practice of net settling similar contracts.
The contract is not readily convertible to cash or net cash settled.
