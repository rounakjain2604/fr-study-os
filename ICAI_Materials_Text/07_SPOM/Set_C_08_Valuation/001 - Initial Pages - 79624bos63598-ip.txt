         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The Board of Studies, ICAI created the study materials for Self-Paced Online Module-Set C with the
goal of aligning the curriculum with the International Education Standards (IESs) of the International
Federation of Accountants (IFAC) to instill and strengthen the prerequisite skills required to become
a well-rounded, capable, and globally competitive accounting professional.
Additionally, this study guide emphasizes the National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020) initiatives,
such as curriculum  restructuring and new pedagogical approaches centered on the use  of
technology in the classroom, as well as conceptual clarity as an alternative to rote learning.
Our economic policies have been liberalized and globalized to be in line with the changes occurring
throughout the world. This has led to the convergence of several significant developments in the
field of Valuation that are required to boost the economy as well as the corporate sector.

Requirement of Professional Knowledge and Skills

It is required of you to not only gain professional knowledge after Intermediate Level but also the
capacity to put that information to use in addressing challenges and solving real-world scenarios
prior to appearing in Final Level. You can acquire the professional values, ethics, and attitudes, as
well as the needed technical competence and professional abilities, by pursuing an integrated
learning approach that combines academic education with hands-on training.

Significant Components of Valuation

Elective Paper of Set C – Valuation is a blend of theory and practical. The Study Material consists
of Chapter 1 to 6 as mentioned in the syllabus.
The study material is prepared by keeping in mind the current scenarios in the valuation, wherever
required. Similarly, students have also been familiarized with the various concepts of valuation. In
the chapter, Introduction to Valuation, the valuation under present scenarios has been discussed
along with the various terminologies used in valuation.
Moreover,  in the Chapter, Need and Purpose of Valuation, besides the need and purpose,
regulatory, tax, transaction based and other requirements-based valuation have been  lucidly
explained.
Furthermore, in the chapter Valuation Approaches and Methodologies, various approaches and
methods along with suitable examples have also been included to give readers a working knowledge

(a)   To develop conceptual understanding of valuation, various approaches, and methodologies.
