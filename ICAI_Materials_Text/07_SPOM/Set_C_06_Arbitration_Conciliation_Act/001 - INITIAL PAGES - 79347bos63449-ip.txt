         BEFORE WE BEGIN…..


The contents of the Study Material for Self–paced Online Module–Set C have been designed and
developed by the Board of Studies, ICAI, with a view to lay the base for future specialisation in the
domain pertaining to the Arbitration and Conciliation Law. The Study Material has been developed
considering  National  Education  Policy  2020  (NEP  2020)  recommendations  emphasizing
conceptual  clarity  rather than  rote  learning.   Emphasis  is  also placed on development  of
application and analytical skills through solving Multiple Choice Questions at the end of each
chapter.
As Chartered Accountants are involved in various financial transactions and business dealings,
they  often  encounter  commercial  disputes  arising  from  contractual  agreements,  financial
transactions, or partnership arrangements. Also, with the globalization of business, Chartered
Accountants  frequently  encounter  international  commercial  disputes  involving  cross-border
transactions or multinational corporations. The Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 provides
Chartered Accountants  with a  robust  legal framework  for  resolving such  disputes through
Arbitration and  Conciliation,  offering an  alternative  to  traditional  litigation.  Arbitration and
Conciliation proceedings conducted under the Act are typically faster, more flexible, and less
formal than court proceedings. Consequently, the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 has been
incorporated as an elective subject within Self-paced Online Module in Set C, aiming to equip
aspiring Chartered Accountants with the requisite knowledge and skills to lay the base for future
specialization in this niche area.
The syllabus of this paper on the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 comprises of Introduction,
Fundamentals of Arbitration, Enforcement of Certain Foreign Awards and Conciliation.
Requirement of Professional Knowledge and Skills

Students are required to learn and qualify Self-paced Online Modules after passing Intermediate
Examination but before appearing in Final Examination. Accordingly, they are expected to acquire
professional knowledge but also the ability to apply such knowledge in addressing issues and
problem solving. The integrated process of learning through academic education and practical
training will help inculcate in them, the requisite technical competence and professional skills.

         SYLLABUS – SELF PACED

                      SET C
     PAPER – 6: THE ARBITRATION AND CONCILIATION ACT, 1996
                              (100 MARKS)
Objective:
To develop an understanding of the Arbitration and Conciliation Law and acquire the  ability of
understanding issues in practical situations.

Contents:
1.     Introduction: Introduction to the various modes of Alternate Dispute Redressal Mechanism
      (ADRs).
2.    Fundamentals of Arbitration: General provisions,  Arbitration Agreement, Conduct  of
        Arbitral Proceedings, Making of Arbitral Award and Termination of Proceedings, Recourse
       against Arbitral Award, Finality and Enforcement of Arbitral Awards.
3.    Enforcement of Certain Foreign Awards: Types of Arbitration, Arbitral Awards (Adhoc,
      Domestic and International Commercial Awards), Enforcement of Foreign  Awards  (New
      York Convention Awards and Geneva Convention Awards).
4.     Conciliation:  Difference  between  Arbitration  and  Conciliation,  Commencement  of
       Conciliation Proceedings, Number of Conciliators, Appointment of Conciliators, Submission
       of statements  to  Conciliator,  Conciliator  not bound by  Certain Enactments, Role  of
       Conciliator, Administrative Assistance, Communication between Conciliator and Parties,
       Disclosure of Information.

include the corresponding provisions of such new legislation with effect from a date
notifiedbytheInstitute.
