     1.1 BUSINESS RESPONSIBILITY AND SUSTAINABILITY
      REPORT ISSUED BY COMMITTEE ON BUSINESS
       RESPONSIBILITY  REPORTING  OF  MINISTRY  OF
      CORPORATE AFFAIRS
Globally, Sustainability Reporting has been gaining momentum and the regulatory framework is
being strengthened.  In Indian context, Ministry  of Corporate  Affairs had issued 'Voluntary
Guidelines on Corporate Social Responsibility’  in 2009 which  laid down the foundation of
mainstreaming the concept of business responsibility. Subsequently, the United Nations Human
Rights Council (UNHRC) adopted the United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human
Rights (UNGPs)  in June, 2011. Endorsing the same, MCA issued the 'National Voluntary
Guidelines on Social, Environmental and Economic Responsibilities of Business, 2011’ (NVGs).
The NVGs contained a Business Responsibility Reporting (BRR) framework which provided
disclosure format for environmental, social and governance aspects of an entity.
Taking this forward, in 2012 the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) through the Listing
Agreement mandated the top 100 listed companies by market capitalisation to  file Business
Responsibility Reports. BRR disclosures were based on the NVGs and intended to enable
businesses to engage more meaningfully with their stakeholders, and encouraged companies to
go beyond regulatory financial compliance and report on their social and environmental impacts.
The requirement  for  filing BRRs was extended to the top 500 listed companies by market
capitalisation from the financial year 2015-16. In March 2019, the updated NVGs were released
as the ‘National Guidelines for Responsible Business Conduct’ (NGRBCs). In December 2019,
SEBI extended the BRR requirement to the top 1000 listed companies by market capitalisation,
from the financial year 2019-2020.
The regulatory push for businesses to provide responsibility and sustainability information also
came with certain provisions in The Companies Act 2013. This Act mandatorily which required
companies to furnish non-financial. Significantly, Section 166 of the Act casts fiduciary duties on
Directors for the same. It required them to promote the objects of the company for the benefit of
its members as a whole, and in the best interests of the company, its employees, the shareholders,
the community and for the protection of environment. Section 135 of the Act introduced provisions
on Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) which were applicable to all the companies having
turnover of Rs 1000 crore or more or net profit of Rs. 5 crore or more or net worth of Rs 500 crore
or more in immediately preceding financial year. Such type of companies were required to spend
in every financial year at least two percent of the average net profits of the company made during
the three immediately preceding financial years on CSR activities listed down in Schedule VII of
the Companies Act.

In 2018, Ministry of Corporate Affairs had constituted Committee on Business Responsibility
Reporting  for preparing Business  Responsibility Reporting formats  for  listed and  unlisted
companies. The disclosure format based on National Voluntary Guidelines (NVGs), namely, the
Business Responsibility Report (BRR) needed to be modified to reflect the principles of National
Guidelines for Responsible Business Conduct (NGRBC). The Committee had been entrusted with
this task as there were various sustainability frameworks being developed globally which were
seeking businesses to be responsible and sustainable towards their environment and society have
been framed and so a new framework was required to be developed. The Committee had analysed
the filings being made by the top 500 companies in the SEBI-BRR; studied the prevalent non-
financial/sustainability frameworks already being used by companies for making disclosures; and
examined the need for a comprehensive non-financial/sustainability reporting requirement by
companies for preparing the proposed formats.
Major global frameworks studied were United Nations Global Compact (UNGC), Global Reporting
Initiative (GRI), Integrated Reporting (IR), Carbon Disclosure Project (CDP), ISO 26000, and
Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB). Brief details of Global Standards/ Frameworks
as given in “Report of the Committee on Business Responsibility Reporting”1 released by MCA

of completeness, accuracy, and clarity of information provided in BRR disclosures of sample of
490 listed companies. The Committee noted BRR framework had created interest amongst
companies towards business responsibility and sustainability. However, further improvements
were required as the BRR disclosures were weak on clarity and in terms of accuracy. The study
also revealed that there was a variance in BRR disclosures across different principles and
highlighted need for seeking specific quantitative data for enabling comparability.
The Committee had followed certain principles for developing the new formats which were named
as “Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting” (BRSR) formats. It had taken existing
Business Responsibility Reporting Framework in Chapter 5 of NGRBCs as the base document.
The Committee had examined the NGRBC-BRR framework in the context of the current BRR filing
made by the companies. It had also considered United Nations Sustainable Development Goals
(SDGs), United Nations Global Compacts (UNGPs) and the prevalent non-financial reporting
frameworks to formulate a holistic, all-encompassing format covering all the essential elements of
non-financial sustainability reporting. The purpose was to develop formats which would serve as
a single comprehensive source of non-financial, sustainability information relevant to all business
stakeholders, viz., investors, shareholders, regulators, and public at large. Accordingly, the BRSR
formats developed were simple, and mindful of the burden and cost of compliance by businesses
so as not be onerous or repetitive.
The Committee recommended two formats. BRSR Comprehensive format for listed companies
and BRSR lite format for unlisted companies. The Committee decided that the information sought
in the new formats should be a mix of quantitative and qualitative data. Quantitative data would
be useful for measurement and comparability of disclosures across companies, sectors, and in
time. Qualitative data will provide an insight on the unique ways in which the companies would be
implementing responsible business conduct in their business activities. The Committee developed
new formats as questionnaires which allowed businesses to easily disclose material aspects.
Further, these formats had been developed in such a way that they can be filled electronically and
integrated with the MCA21 database of Ministry of Corporate Affairs. The Committee had also
prepared Guidance Note which defined and interpreted the scope of each question to enable
reporting by companies. The usage of terms has been made consistent with the Companies Act,
2013, any other prevalent statute(s), and NGRBC.

     1.2 SECURITIES EXCHANGE BOARD OF INDIA  (SEBI)
       CIRCULAR ON  BUSINESS  RESPONSIBILITY AND
       SUSTAINABILITY REPORTING (BRSR)
In August 2020, SEBI issued “Consultation Paper on the Format of Business Responsibility and
Sustainability Reporting”2 This consultation paper invited comments from various stakeholders on
the BRSR formats recommended in MCA “Report of the Committee on Business Responsibility
and  Sustainability  Reporting”.  Thereafter  in May,  2021, SEBI  introduced new  reporting
requirements on ESG parameters called the Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report
(BRSR) by virtue of amendment to regulation 34 (2)  (f) of LODR Regulations vide Gazette
notification no. SEBI/LAD-NRO/GN/2021/22 dated May 05, 2021.
SEBI Circular “Business responsibility and sustainability reporting by listed entities”3 dated May
10, 2021, introduced BRSR formats in place of BRR formats. As per the Circular, new BRSR
format require disclosures from listed entities (covered under the applicability criteria) on their
performance against the nine principles of the ‘National Guidelines on Responsible Business
Conduct’ (NGBRCs). Further, the circular prescribes reporting under each principle to be divided
into essential indicators and leadership indicators. The essential indicators are required to be
reported on a mandatory basis while the reporting of leadership indicators is on a voluntary basis.
The Circular encourages listed entities to report the leadership indictors also which are forward
looking best practices.
With respect to applicability of new BRSR formats, aforesaid Circular laid down that with effect
from the financial year 2022-2023, filing of BRSR shall be mandatory for the top 1000 listed
companies by market capitalization. BRSR formats will be replacing the current BRR formats.
However, the Circular encouraged companies to file BRSR instead of BRR voluntarily for the
financial year 2021-2022 also. This would provide early adopters initial time to set their processes
and operations in accordance with the requirements of new formats.
In July, 2023, SEBI has issued Circular4 on “BRSR Core – Framework for Assurance and ESG
Disclosures for Value Chain”. Accordingly, BRSR format after incorporating new KPIs of BRSR

SEBI circular specifies that the BRSR format prescribes quantitative and standardized disclosures
on ESG parameters. This would enable comparability  of  sustainability disclosures across
companies, sectors and also over time. The purpose is to make ESG disclosures helpful for
investors enabling them to make better investment decisions. The added advantage of BRSR is
that through these disclosures companies can have more meaningful engagement with their
stakeholders. Stakeholders can be encouraged to look beyond financial information and also give
importance to social and environmental impacts created by the activities of the company. In case
the  listed  entities  are  already  preparing and  disclosing  sustainability  reports based on
internationally accepted reporting frameworks like, Global Reporting Initiative (GRI), Sustainability
Accounting Standards Board (SASB), Task Force on Climate Related Financial Disclosures
(TCFD) or Integrated Reporting. The circular allows cross-reference of the ESG disclosures made
under such framework to the disclosures which are required under the BRSR format. The
companies can prepare a mapping document that can show disclosures made as per International
frameworks by the company with respective requirement of BRSR disclosures.

                              Social, Environmental, and Economic Responsibilities.

     2015: To initiate the process of updating the (NVGs) to be in line with the United Nations Sustainable
    Development Goals (SDGs), Business Responsibility Reporting (BRR) was expanded to include the top

                       goes beyond simply complying with legal requirements.

      mandatory for the top 1000 listed companies by market capitalization. This framework incorporates
           elements from leading sustainability reporting standards like SASB, TCFD, and GRI etc.

       transfer of essential indicators to the leadership indicators of Section C (Principle wise performance).

                        chain introduced post incorporating new KPIs and disclosures.

     1.3 BUSINESS RESPONSIBILITY AND SUSTAINABILITY
      REPORTING (BRSR) DISCLOSURES
SEBI  Circular  “Business  responsibility and  sustainability  reporting by  listed  entities” No.
SEBI/HO/CFD/CMD-2/P/CIR/2021/562 dated May 10, 2021, prescribes the format of Business
Responsibility & Sustainability Report. As per Annexure I of the Circular, BRSR format consists
of three sections.
1.     Section A relates to the General Disclosures of the business and requires details of listed
        entity, Products/Services, List of Operations, Market served by the entity, Details about
      employees, Holding, Subsidiary and Associate Companies, Corporate Social Responsibility
     (CSR) and Transparency & Disclosure Compliances.
2.     Section B is on Management and Process disclosures. This section is aimed at helping
      businesses demonstrate the structures, policies and processes put  in place towards
      adopting the NGRBC Principles and Core Elements. It consists of disclosure questions to
      help the businesses  to explain  their Policy and Management Processes as well as
      Governance, Leadership and Oversight, highlighting ESG related challenges, targets and
      achievements.
3.     Section C provides the disclosure of principle wise performance of the businesses across
       the  nine  principles  of NGRBC. These  principles  are  the  basic framework  for  the
       organisations  to  report  their  contribution  for  sustainable development through  their
        activities. Information under each Principle is categorized into “Essential” and “Leadership”
       indicators. Disclosure  of information  for  essential  indicators  is mandatory  for every
      business that has adopted NGRBC. However, the disclosure of information for leadership
       indicators is voluntarily. It is encouraging businesses which want to progress to a higher
       level of adoption of socially, environmentally and ethically responsible business practices.
SEBI Circular “BRSR Core – Framework for assurance and ESG disclosures for value chain” No.
SEBI/HO/CFD/CFD-SEC-2/P/CIR/2023/122 dated July 12, 2023, prescribes the updated format
of BRSR core to be adopted by Top 1000 listed entities and its assurance in the phased manner.

SECTION A – GENERAL DISCLOSURES
Section A of the BRSR format is on “General Disclosures”. This section has been divided into
seven parts.
Part 1 deals with “Details of the listed Entity”. The information requested in this Part I is related
to the legal status of the organisation. This section provides all the basic information about the
listed entity in brief snapshot Information sought under this section along with explanations are
as follows:
1.    Corporate Identity Number (CIN) of the Listed Entity - This is the number issued by the
       Registrar of Companies at the time of Registration under Companies Act, 2013. Every
     company is allocated a unique number.
2.   Name of the Listed Entity – This is the registered name by which the entity has been issued
       Certificate of Incorporation from the Registrar of Companies.
3.    Year of Incorporation – This is the year in which the company was incorporated and had
      received the Certificate of Incorporation from the Registrar of Companies.
4.    Registered office address - Registered office address means the official address at which
       the company shall be able to receive all official letters sent by any government or non-
      government or regulatory body.
5.    Corporate address  - Corporate address is the place where company is conducting  its
      operation and is being used for business purpose.
6.     E-mail: It is the Official E-mail-id used for communication purpose by the business.
7.    Telephone: It can either be landline or Mobile number which is being used by the company
        for official communication.
8.    Website: It is the official website of the company which contains all the relevant details of
       the company  like, company name, registered  office address, company  identification
      number, telephone number, fax number, etc.
9.     Financial year for which reporting is being done: The company has to specify the financial
      year for which the BRSR report has been prepared. For example, If the report is prepared
        for the period between 1 April, 2023 and 31 March, 2024 then the report must indicate the
       financial year as 2023-2024.
10.   Name of the Stock Exchange(s) where shares are listed:
11.   Paid-up Capital  - The information related to the money received by the company from
      shareholders in exchange for shares.
12.   Name and contact details (telephone, email address) of the person who may be contacted
        in case of any queries on the BRSR report of the company.
