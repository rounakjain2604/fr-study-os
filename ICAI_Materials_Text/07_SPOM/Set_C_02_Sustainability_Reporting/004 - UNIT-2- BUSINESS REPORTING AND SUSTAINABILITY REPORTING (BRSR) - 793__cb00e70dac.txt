     2.1 INTRODUCTION
The MCA “Report of the Committee on Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting”1 had
also recommended smaller version of BRSR called BRSR Lite for the non-listed companies. BRSR
formats notified by SEBI for listed companies were termed as very comprehensive and detailed in
nature. The Committee recognised that at present, only the top 500 listed companies have the
experience of business responsibility reporting. Other companies may thus be unfamiliar with any
form of sustainability reporting and the proposed BRSR would perhaps be their  first effort at
developing a sustainability report.  Therefore, a pared down Lite version of the BRSR format has
been proposed, to make  it easier for all companies to begin reporting on sustainability reporting
related issues.  This again has the Essential and Leadership category of questions, but fewer in
number, and seeks information which such companies should be able to provide. Accordingly, the
Committee recommends that smaller unlisted companies below specified threshold may, to begin
with, adopt a Lite version of the format, on a voluntary basis.

SECTION B – MANAGEMENT AND PROCESS DISCLOSURES
Section B “Management and Process Disclosures” of BRSR Lite Format  is aimed at helping
businesses demonstrate the structures, policies and processes put in place towards adopting the
Principles and Core Elements. Disclosure requirements are as follows –
(a)   The first three clause require mapping of nine NGRBC principles with respective policies of
       the company. It has to be mentioned whether policies have been approved by the Board and
       the web link of the policies. Companies are encouraged to extend their policies to value chain
       partners and also adopt international standards/ frameworks. As discussed in Chapter 1 of
      Module 3, there are various policies framed by a company such as, Code of Conduct, Anti-
       bribery and Anti-Corruption Policy, Anti-Money Laundering Policy, Policy on dealing with
       related parties, Document Retention and Archival Policy, Risk Management Policy.

        (v)   Any other reason (please specify).
SECTION C: PRINCIPLE WISE PERFORMANCE DISCLOSURE
Section C of BRSR Lite is aimed at helping businesses demonstrate their performance in integrating
the Principles and Core Elements with their key processes and decisions. Information required has
been categorized as “Essential” and “Leadership” indicators. Essential indicators are expected to be
disclosed from every business that has adopted these Guidelines. However, leadership indicators
are expected of businesses which aspire to progress to a higher level in their quest to be socially,
environmentally and ethically responsible. Disclosures are required under each of the nine Principles
but are comparatively lesser than BRSR Comprehensive format.

PRINCIPLE 8 Businesses should promote inclusive growth and equitable development
Essential indicator requirement for Principle 8 are as follows –
(a)    With respect to CSR projects undertaken by it, the company has to describe the mechanisms
       established by it to receive and redress grievances of the community.
(b)   The company is required to disclose whether benefits derived of the various intellectual
       properties owned or acquired by it, based on traditional knowledge, been shared equitably.
       Details to be disclosed are as follows -
        Intellectual Property based on traditional knowledge
       Owned/ Acquired
        Benefit shared
(c)   The company has to report list of adverse orders and case details of intellectual property
       rights disputes related to traditional knowledge during the reporting period.
Disclosure under leadership indicators of Principle 8 are as follows:
(a)    With respect to Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) projects undertaken by the company
        in designated aspirational districts as identified by government bodies, disclosure has to be
     made of amount spent in each of them. As per the ‘Transformation of Aspirational Districts’
     programme of the Government, a list of districts has been identified for quick and effective
       transformation.  For additional details, refer to the following link: https://niti.gov.in/about-
       aspirational-districtsprogramme.
(b)    Details of the benefits derived of the various intellectual properties owned or acquired by the
     company based on traditional knowledge shared. Information to be reported is as follows –
        Intellectual Property based on traditional knowledge
       Owned/ Acquired
       Basis of calculating benefit share
(c)     In case there is any negative social impact identified with respect to intellectual property
       related cases wherein usage of traditional knowledge is involved, then the company has to
       report details of corrective actions taken by it.

      complaints and feedback. Generally, companies lay down multiple mechanisms for consumer
      complaints and feedback wherein designated officials acknowledge, analyse the incidents
      and develop an action plan to resolve it for ensuring utmost consumer satisfaction.
