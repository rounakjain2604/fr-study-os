         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The contents of the Study Material for Self–paced Online Module–Set C, of Paper 7 Forensic
Accounting have been designed and developed by the Board of Studies, ICAI, with a view to lay the
foundation for future specialisation in the area of Forensic Accounting. The Study Material has been
developed  considering  the  National  Education  Policy 2020 (NEP  2020) recommendations
emphasizing the conceptual clarity rather than rote learning. Emphasis is also placed on fostering
the application and analytical skills by solving Multiple Choice Questions provided at the end of each
chapter.  In line with the NEP 2020 recommendation on technology-based learning, the Study
Material would be supplemented by e-lectures facilitating the experiential learning.
Forensic Accounting has emerged as critical discipline in today’s complex business environment,
necessitating the establishment of concise Standards to ensure consistency, professionalism and
ethical conduct in this specialized area. The role of Chartered Accountants to uncover financial
irregularities and ensure transparency and accountability has become more crucial than ever. The
Institute  of  Chartered  Accountants  of  India  (ICAI) has  released  Forensic  Accounting and
Investigation Standards (FAISs)  in 2021 to cover the whole process of planning, executing,
concluding and reporting of FAI engagements. These Standards mandate the key requirements to
ensure quality performance and are now mandatory from 1st July, 2023. Thus, it is equally important
that the Chartered Accountancy students should have due understanding of these FAIS.
Under the Revised Scheme of Education and Training, paper of Forensic Accounting has been
introduced under Set C considering the significance of this area in today’s era. Efforts have been
made to present each topic of the syllabus in a lucid manner and care has been taken to present
the chapters in a logical sequence to facilitate easy understanding by the students.  The study
material has been bifurcated into ten chapters in line with the prescribed syllabus. Every effort has
been done to make the Study Material error free, however if inadvertently any error is present and
found by readers, they may send it to us immediately, so that it can be rectified at our end.
In case you need any further clarification/ guidance, you may send your queries through ICAI BoS App.
We hope that these student-friendly features in the Study Material make your learning process more
enjoyable, enrich your knowledge and sharpen your application skills.
Students may please note that Parliament has passed three criminal laws viz. Bhartiya Nyaya
Sanhita, 2023  Bhartiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita,2023 and Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam,
