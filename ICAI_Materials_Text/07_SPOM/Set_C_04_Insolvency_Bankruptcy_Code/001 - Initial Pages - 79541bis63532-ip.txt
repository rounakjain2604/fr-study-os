         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The contents  of the Study Material  for Self–paced Online Module–Set C  of Paper  4: The
Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 have been designed and developed by the Board of
Studies, ICAI, with a view to lay the foundation for future specialisation in the area of insolvency
resolution. The Study Material has been developed considering the National Education Policy 2020
(NEP 2020) recommendations emphasizing the conceptual  clarity  rather than  rote  learning.
Emphasis is also placed on fostering the application and analytical skills by solving Multiple Choice
Questions provided at the end of each chapter.  In line with the NEP 2020 recommendation on
technology-based learning, the Study Material would be supplemented by e-lectures facilitating the
experiential learning.
The syllabus of this paper on the Insolvency and Bankruptcy, 2016, comprises of 7 chapters
covering:
•    The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 and its importance.
•    The  Insolvency  Resolutions  for  Corporate  Persons,  Liquidation  process,  Fast Track
      Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process, Voluntary Liquidation of Corporate Persons, Pre-
      packaged Insolvency Resolution Process.
•    The Insolvency Resolution and Bankruptcy for Individuals and Partnership Firms, Fresh
       Start Process and Distribution of the Estate of the Bankrupt.
•    The  Insolvency and  Bankruptcy  Board  of  India,  Insolvency  Professional  Agencies,
      Insolvency Professionals and Information Utilities.
•      Significant Judicial Pronouncements under The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016.
The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), 2016 was introduced in India to revamp and address
longstanding issues in insolvency and bankruptcy procedures. This legislation has significantly
streamlined the insolvency resolution process, bolstered creditor rights and fostered a culture of
entrepreneurship and risk-taking in India's business environment.
With the implementation of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016, a new professional avenue
has emerged in the form of Insolvency Professionals (IPs). These professionals play a pivotal role
in the insolvency and bankruptcy process, undertaking a diverse range of responsibilities including
legal  compliance,  management,  as  well  as  financial  and  accounting  functions.  IPs  are
indispensable  pillars ensuring  the  effective,  timely  functioning and  credibility  of  the  entire
insolvency resolution mechanism.

           SYLLABUS

          PAPER-4: THE INSOLVENCY AND BANKRUPTCY
                  CODE, 2016 (100 MARKS)


Objective
To develop an understanding of the Code and acquire the ability to apply such knowledge in
practical situations.
Contents
The Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016 covering references of Significant Rules and
Regulations:
1.   Introduction covering Journey of development of Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code and its
     importance.
2.   Preliminary: Relevant Definitions and application of the Code.
3.   Insolvency Resolutions and Liquidation for Corporate Persons:  Corporate Insolvency
     Resolution Process, Liquidation Process, Pre-Packaged Insolvency Resolution Process, Fast
     Track Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process, Voluntary Liquidation of Corporate Persons,
     Adjudicating Authority for Corporate Persons.
4.   Insolvency  Resolution  and  Bankruptcy  for  Individuals  and  Partnership  Firms:
     Preliminary (Application & Definitions), Fresh Start Process, Insolvency Resolution Process,
     Bankruptcy Order for Individuals & Partnership Firms, Administration & Distribution of the
     Estate of the Bankrupt, Adjudicating Authority, Offences & Penalties.
5.   Regulation  of  Insolvency  Professionals, Agencies and  Information  Utilities: The
     Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India, Powers & Functions of the Board, Insolvency
     Professional  Agencies,  Insolvency  Professionals,  Information   Utilities,  Inspection  &
      Investigation, Finance, Accounts & Audit.
6.   Miscellaneous provisions including Forms 1-6 filed for initiation of CIRP before the NCLT,
       lists of all rules and regulations under IBC.
7.   Significant Judicial Pronouncements under Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016.
Note:Ifnewlegislationsareenactedinplaceoftheexistinglegislations,thesyllabuswould
include the corresponding provisions of such new legislations with effect from a date
notifiedbytheInstitute.
The specific inclusions/exclusions in the various topics covered in the syllabus will be
effected every year by way of Study Guidelines, if required.
