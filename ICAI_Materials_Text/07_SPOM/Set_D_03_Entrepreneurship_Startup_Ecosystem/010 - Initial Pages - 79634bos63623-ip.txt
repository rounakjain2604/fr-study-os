         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The  contents  of  the  Study  Material  for  Self–paced  Online  Module–Set D  of  Paper  3:
Entrepreneurship and Start-Up Ecosystem have been designed and developed by the Board of
Studies, ICAI, with a view to provide an overview in the area of Entrepreneurship and Start-Up
Ecosystem. The Self–paced Online Modules of Set-D focus on progressing towards a more holistic
and multi-disciplinary education as emphasized in the National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020)
for developing  intellectual, aesthetic,  social, physical, emotional and moral capacities of the
Chartered Accountancy students in an integrated manner. The Study Material has been developed
considering the NEP 2020 recommendations emphasizing conceptual  clarity rather than rote
learning. Emphasis is also placed on fostering the application and analytical skills by solving Multiple
Choice Questions provided at the end of each chapter.  In line with the NEP 2020 recommendation
on technology-based learning, the Study Material would be supplemented by e-lectures facilitating
the experiential learning.
This study material is like a guiding light for anyone interested in starting their own business, it is
not just about starting a company, it is about thinking innovatively, solving problems, and striving for
excellence. By reading this study material, you will learn the basics of entrepreneurship and what it
takes to build something from scratch. You will discover how to create a compelling pitch to attract
investors and how to explain your business idea quickly and effectively.
Entrepreneurship involves taking risks and embracing uncertainty. You will learn how to navigate
these challenges and explore the Indian startup scene, with its unique opportunities and obstacles.
The book also explains key terms used in the startup world, like unicorns (companies valued at over
a billion dollars), bootstrapping (starting a company with minimal resources), and revenue models.
Understanding these terms will help you speak the language of entrepreneurship. You will also learn
about the importance of incubators and accelerators, which help startups grow by providing
mentorship and resources.
Throughout the study material, you will be inspired by quotes from successful entrepreneurs like
Elon Musk and Richard Branson. Their words will encourage you to believe in yourself and pursue
your dreams. In short, this study material is your roadmap to entrepreneurship, guiding you through
the challenges and opportunities of starting your own business.

Examination but before appearing in Final Examination. Accordingly, they are expected to not only
acquire professional knowledge but also the ability to apply such knowledge in addressing issues

               SYLLABUS

                        SET – D
    PAPER-3: ENTREPRENEURSHIP AND START-UP ECOSYSTEM
                             (100 MARKS)

Objective
To gain knowledge and acquire the ability to develop multidimensional skills in Entrepreneurship
and Start-Up Ecosystem.
Contents
Unit  I: Part A: Introduction to Entrepreneurship & Startups: Meaning and concept  of
entrepreneurship & Startups, the history of entrepreneurship development, Types of entrepreneurs,
Role of entrepreneurship & Startups in economic development, Rise of Startups: Global Perspective,
Rise of Startups: Indian Perspective. the skills/ traits required to be an entrepreneur, creative and
design Thinking, the entrepreneurial decision process, Succession Planning, Managing Family
Business.
Part B: Startup Registration: DPIIT Recognition, Benefits, Government Seed Funding etc.
Unit II: Crafting business models and Lean Start-ups: Introduction to business models; Creating
value propositions-conventional industry logic, value innovation logic; customer focused innovation;
building and analysing business models; Business model canvas, Introduction to lean start-ups,
Business Pitching.
Unit  III: Product Development: Building a Minimum Viable Product (MVP), Journey towards
building "Product Market Fit", Practical case studies on journey of successful startups from MVP to
PMF, Prototype and Agile development methodology for tech Startups, Identify critical success
elements: Product (tech), Product (offering), Procurement, Supply Chain
Unit IV: Pitching the idea and presentation: Drafting of Pitch Deck and Business Plan, Art of
Elevator Pitch, Rules/Steps of Pitching the idea, Art of Persuasion
Unit V: Institutions Supporting Small Business Enterprises: Central level institutions. State level
institutions. Other agencies.  Industry  Associations. Current government schemes supporting
entrepreneurship

Unit VI: Funding & Valuation: Understanding basics of Startup Funding & where to get investment,
Quantifying the seed capital, Prerequisite of Startup Funding, Financial Modelling for the Startups,
How to approach Angel Investors & VC Firms, understanding Term Sheet, Investor Agreement and
Equity Dilution, the science behind 'Startup Valuation'
Unit VII: Legal considerations for a Startup: Choosing an ideal entity type and overview of
registration process, Startup Equity Split among cofounders and equity vesting, ESOP Planning and
other best practices, Drafting of Cofounder Agreement, Registering intellectual property rights (IPRs)
[Patent, Copyright and Trademark etc.],
Unit VIII: Startup Incubators & Acceleration:  Difference between incubators and accelerators,
Choosing the right one (factors and effect thereof), Working of incubators and accelerators
UNIT IX: Miscellaneous: Case studies on success stories, Role of Startup Advisors & Funding
Agents, Building a right Startup Team, Founder's grooming: Role of PR Building Activities in success
of startups, Being part of Startup Community and leading it and Exit Strategies
