         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The contents of the Study Material for Self–paced Online Module–Set D of  Paper -1: The
Constitution of India and Art of Advocacy have been designed and developed by the Board of
Studies, ICAI, with a view to provide an overview of the Indian Constitution and having an
understanding of legal system and basic principles of drafting and pleading. The Self–paced
Online Modules of Set-D focus on progressing towards a more holistic and multi-disciplinary
education as emphasized in the National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020) for developing
intellectual,  aesthetic,  social, physical, emotional and moral capacities  of the Chartered
Accountancy students  in an integrated manner.  The Study Material has been developed
considering the NEP 2020 recommendations emphasizing the conceptual clarity rather than rote
learning. Emphasis is also placed on fostering the application and analytical skills by solving
Multiple Choice Questions provided at the end of each chapter.  In line with the NEP 2020
recommendation on technology-based learning, the Study Material would be supplemented by
e-lectures facilitating the experiential learning.
The Indian Constitution holds immense significance as the cornerstone of the nation's democratic
framework, embodying the collective aspirations, values, and principles of its diverse population.
The Constitution through extensive deliberation and debate, it not only provides the legal foundation
for governance but also serves as a beacon of justice, equality, and rights protection.
The  Art  of Advocacy holds a great significance  in the  legal profession, serving as the
cornerstone  of  justice,  fairness, and  the  protection  of  rights  within  society. Advocacy
comprehends a diverse set of skills and principles that enable legal professionals to represent
their clients effectively, uphold the rule of law, and navigate complex legal processes with
integrity and diligence.
The syllabus of this paper on the Constitution of India and Art of Advocacy comprises of two
Parts. Part  I deals with the Constitution of India and Part  II with the Art of Advocacy. The
Constitution of India contains six chapters broadly covering about the Constitutional history and
its development, Preamble, the other  constitutional provisions related the Union and  its
Territory, Citizenship, Fundamental Rights, Duties and Directive Principles, Government of India
and States, Centre-State relationship, Emergency Provisions and procedure for Amendments.
The Art of Advocacy is comprising of three chapters covering several aspects, including the
evolution of India's legal system, the representation provided by Chartered Accountants in

               SYLLABUS

                         SET - D
       PAPER – 1: THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA AND ART OF
                 ADVOCACY (100 MARKS)
Objective:
(a)     To have basic knowledge of the Constitution governing the State, Government and rights
       and duties of the Citizens.
(b)     To develop an understanding of the legal system in India and acquire knowledge and apply
        basic principles of drafting and pleading.
             PART I: THE CONSTITUTION OF INDIA
Contents:
1.      Introduction: Preamble, Features, Sources, the Union and its Territory, Citizenship,
       Fundamental Rights, Duties and Directive Principles.
2.     Government of Union: The Executive, Parliament, Judiciary.
3.     Government of States: The Executive, State Legislature, High courts in the States.
4.      Relation between Union and the States: Legislative relations, Administrative
         relations, Financial relations.
5.     Emergency Provisions
6.     Procedure for Amendments
                PART II: ART OF ADVOCACY
1.     Development of Legal System in India
2.      Representation by Chartered Accountants in Tribunals
3.      Basic Rules of drafting and pleading
Note: If new legislations are enacted in place of the existing legislations, the syllabus
would include the corresponding provisions of such new legislations with effect from a
date notified by the Institute.
The specific inclusions/exclusions in the various topics covered in the syllabus will be
effected every year by way of Study Guidelines, if required.
