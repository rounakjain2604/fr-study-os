         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The contents of the Study Material for Self–paced Online Module–Set C of Paper 1 – Risk Management
have been designed and developed by the Board of Studies, ICAI, with a view to lay the foundation for
future specialisation in the area of Risk Management.  The Study Material has been developed
considering the National Education Policy 2020 (NEP 2020) recommendations emphasizing the
conceptual clarity rather than rote learning. Emphasis is also placed on fostering the application and
analytical skills by solving Multiple Choice Questions provided at the end of each chapter.  In line with
the NEP 2020  recommendation on  technology-based  learning,  the  Study  Material  would be
supplemented by e-lectures facilitating the experiential learning.
Though the topic of Risk Management has been important since the evolution of the business but its
importance has been considerably increased post 2008 crisis and pandemic in 2020. The objective of
this paper on Risk Management is to gain knowledge and acquire an insight into the spectrum of risks
faced by businesses and to develop an understanding of the techniques of managing risks in practical
case scenarios.
Requirement of Professional Knowledge and Skills

Students are required to learn and qualify Self-paced Online Modules after passing Intermediate
Examination but before appearing in Final Examination. Accordingly, they are expected to not only
acquire professional knowledge but also the ability to apply such knowledge in addressing issues
and problem solving. The integrated process of learning through academic education and practical
training will help inculcate in them, the requisite technical competence and professional skills.
Framework of Chapters: Uniform Structure comprising of specific components

Efforts have been made to present the content of Risk Management in a lucid manner. Care has
been taken to present the chapters in a logical sequence to facilitate easy understanding by the
students. Each chapter of the Study Material has been structured uniformly and comprises of the
following components:
