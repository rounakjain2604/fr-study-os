Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) is a dynamic field, and emerging issues continually shape the
landscape.
Here are some potential emerging issues in ERM:

     1. EXTERNAL INFLUENCES
External influences play a critical role in Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) as they contribute to
the dynamic and ever-changing risk landscape that organizations must navigate.
Here are ways in which external influences become issues in ERM:
1.1 Climate Change and Environmental Risks
The continuous rise  in global population means that we as human need to find increasingly
imaginative ways to survive together. No longer can we afford to destroy parts of our surrounding
ecosystems for short-term gain, as that destruction will likely impact someone else, and what is
lost may well have proven valuable if we had nurtured it.
The recognition of climate change as a systemic risk is growing. Companies are increasingly
expected to assess and disclose their exposure to climate-related risks. ERM frameworks may
need to incorporate climate risk assessments to ensure long-term sustainability.
1.2 Supply Chain Disruptions
Global events, such as the COVID-19 pandemic, highlighted vulnerabilities in supply chains. ERM
must address the risks associated with supply chain disruptions, including geopolitical tensions,
trade conflicts, and natural disasters.

The pace of innovation and growth in business is incredible, and the slightest disruption can open
the door for competitors to capture your clients, recruit your  staff and overtake your market
position. Increasingly, companies are using outsourcing not only as a means of reducing costs, but
also to get access to specialised knowledge and skills, to increase their production flexibility or to
deliver superior products. As a consequence, the dependence on suppliers increases, as does the
risk of revenue shortfall when a supply chain breaks down.
1.3 Social Media and Reputation Risks
Social media can significantly impact an organization's reputation. Managing reputational risks
arising from social media requires proactive strategies and monitoring to address potential issues
before they escalate.
Reputational risk is most relevant, in a commercial context, which  it potentially impairs current or
prospective earnings arising from the non- delivery of the “promise”. Even where there is no direct
financial impact  (e.g.in a  public  sector  or  not-for-profit  organisation),  the  crystallisation  of
reputational risk increases the transaction costs of doing business because of the time, energy and
cost involved in restoring trust and confidence among stakeholders.
1.4 Regulatory Changes
Organizations navigate a multifaceted landscape  of  legal challenges  within  their operational
sphere. Compliance obligations span across borders, necessitating a nuanced grasp of diverse
legal frameworks. Legal risks stem from various origins such as contractual obligations, litigation
exposure,  liability  concerns, and  regulatory compliance  disparities.  Decision-making  entails
substantial uncertainty, given the potential legal ramifications. Effective management of legal risk
is instrumental in safeguarding and augmenting organizational value.
Changes  in regulatory environments can have a profound impact on an organization's  risk
landscape. ERM frameworks must be adaptable to evolving regulations to ensure compliance and
avoid penalties.
1.5 Political and Geopolitical Risks
Political instability and geopolitical tensions can impact businesses operating in different regions.
ERM should assess the potential risks arising from political developments, trade policies, and
international relations.

Political risk describes the potential challenges to a company or a specific operation arising from
government behaviour and exposure to conflicts and rivalries between different social groups, and
between government and their opponents (whether domestic or other governments).
Globalisation has created greater interconnectedness and, by implication, greater vulnerability to
events beyond the boundaries of sovereign territory and far beyond our control. Though the search
for greater economic rewards and economies  of scale presented by globalisation  is  clearly
understood, the concomitant impact of heightened exposure to political risk is often ignored.
This  is very  significant  to consider the tremendous influence that  politics exerts on market
movements and how the most unexpected economic events are often triggered by political action
or inaction.
Anticipating the risks associated with each of these trends requires understanding the political and
economic environment in each territory ,the separation  of powers and the independence of the
various branches of state, institutional independence, the prevalence of the rule of law, and the
preferences of leaders who determine policy choices and thereby  political and economic out
comes .Often a lack of Political capacity or willingness to maintain a stable society and predictable
economic environment leads to financial loss.

      2. GLOBAL DEVELOPMENTS
Global development, characterized by economic, technological, and social advancements on a
global scale, introduces both opportunities and challenges for Enterprise Risk Management (ERM).
The pace of global development can lead to the emergence of new and unexpected risks that
organizations may  not have encountered  before,  requiring a  proactive and  adaptive ERM
approach.
Here are some of the issues and challenges that may arise due to global development in the
context of ERM:
2.1 Increased Complexity of Risks
Organizations operating globally face a more complex risk landscape with interconnected and
interdependent risks across various regions and markets. Global supply chains introduce risks
related to geopolitical events, trade tensions, and disruptions that can affect the flow of goods and
services.

2.2 Regulatory Compliance Challenges
Organizations operating globally must navigate a diverse range of regulatory environments, each
with its own set of rules and compliance requirements, leading to increased challenges in ERM
compliance.
2.3 Cultural and Communication Barriers
Operating  in  diverse  cultural environments can  lead  to  challenges  in communication and
understanding of risk perceptions. ERM needs to account for cultural nuances and communication
challenges.
2.4 Political and Geopolitical Risks
Organizations face  risks related  to  political  instability, changes  in government  policies, and
geopolitical tensions, which can impact business operations and strategies on a global scale.
2.5 Environmental and Climate Risks
Global development often contributes to environmental challenges and climate change, introducing
risks related to regulatory changes, physical impacts, and shifts in consumer preferences.
2.6 Data Privacy and Protection
With increased data exchange globally, organizations face challenges in complying with data
protection regulations and ensuring the privacy and security of sensitive information.
2.7 Market Volatility
Global economic developments, such as  financial crises or economic downturns  in  different
regions, can impact organizations and necessitate a robust ERM strategy to manage financial and
economic risks.
2.8 Supply Chain Risks
The phenomenon of globalization has resulted in increasingly intricate and widespread supply
networks. Any disturbances occurring in one geographical area can reverberate throughout the
entire supply chain. Consequently, Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) needs to carefully assess
these risks associated with global supply chains and formulate plans to bolster resilience.

2.9 Crisis Management
Global events, such as natural disasters, pandemics, or political crises, can have widespread
effects on  businesses. ERM  should  include  robust  crisis management  plans  to  address
unexpected events that may have a global impact.
To address these issues, organizations need to enhance their ERM frameworks by adopting a
more comprehensive and  adaptive approach.  This  includes  staying  informed about  global
developments, leveraging technology  for  risk monitoring and analysis, fostering a risk-aware
culture, and building agility into their risk management processes to respond effectively to changes
in the global landscape.
In recent years, firms have launched reviews of their operational and governance models to
address weakness considered to have contributed to the increased and often unrecognised risk.
These reviews address a wide range of areas, including product complexity, that made risks
difficult to assess, as well as incentive schemes and oversight.
Furthermore, companies, advisors and regulators around the world have an increased focus on
Risk culture. In the wake of the financial crisis, banks and insurers have been working to improve
their risk governance structures and, by extension, their risk culture.
2.10 Impact of Global Recession on ERM
The Western world has some of the most sophisticated financial systems and also regulations to
monitor these systems. But for some reasons, Wall-Street’s philandering into exotic and highly
risky financial products (such as Sub- Prime Lending) was not foreseen by the regulators in a
robust manner so as to prevent its devastating impact. All in all, the global financial meltdown is
such a catastrophic event which the regulators are charged with preventing, yet they don’t even
seem to have done a good job of detecting its onslaught.

    3. TECHNOLOGY & AUTOMATION
3.1 Technological Innovations
Rapid technological advancements, such as  artificial intelligence, blockchain, and automation,
present both  opportunities and  risks. ERM needs  to assess the  potential impact  of these
technologies on business processes, security, and regulatory compliance.

3.2 Data Privacy and Compliance
Regulatory landscapes related to data privacy (e.g. Digital Personal Data Protection Act) are
continually evolving. Organizations need to navigate these complexities and ensure compliance
with data protection regulations, requiring a robust risk management approach.
3.3 Remote Work and Cybersecurity
The increasing frequency and sophistication of cyber threats pose a significant challenge for
organizations. ERM needs to adapt to the evolving nature of cybersecurity risks and develop
strategies to mitigate potential damage.
The shift to remote work has introduced new challenges, especially regarding cybersecurity and
data protection. ERM needs to address the risks associated with a dispersed workforce and
ensure that remote work practices align with security protocols.
In most companies who have reached some level of risk maturity, they have quickly realised that
identifying risk related events is also too late. They have gone one step ahead and started to
institute risk prevention measures whereby they are able to use technology and data analysis in
such an intelligent manner, that the perpetrators can be identified on a real-time basis. They
undertake risk profiling of perpetrators and program into their systems so that as soon as these
individuals enter their systems they are able to monitor and track them in an “online” manner so
that as soon as they commit the crime, they can be nabbed. Banks, Credit Card companies,
Telecom Operators, Retail Department Stores etc are at the forefront of this monitoring.
3.4 Risk of strategic Alliances
Many companies are undertaking  strategic  alliances  to  exploit business  opportunities. The
distinction between vendors and customers is getting blurred. A major IT player can buy a lot of
services from some companies and at the same time provide them a lot of IT solutions. Hence,
they form a cohesive alliance so that they are able to pool their resources in such a way that most
of the buying and selling is undertaken only from their own entities, which allows them to take
advantage of economies of scale. This poses many challenges from a risk perspective.
3.5 Recent Development/ Challenges in Fraud Risk
Satyam Computers has given a big jolt to Corporate India with respect to the size and audacity of
the  fraud which  they  perpetrated.  In  fact,  the shockwaves  are   still  reverberating  despite
government’s swift reaction to control the situation. Apart from the magnitude and complexity of
the fraud, the events leading up to its unfolding  , the fact that the rot was at the helm and the
ability of the perpetrators to disguise  it for so long, has been true wake -up calls  for other
companies.
