         BEFORE WE BEGIN …


The contents of the Study Material for Self–paced Online Module–Set D of Paper 2B have been
designed and developed by the Board of Studies, ICAI, with a view to provide an overview in the
area of Philosophy. The Self–paced Online Modules of Set-D focus on progressing towards a more
holistic and multi-disciplinary education as emphasized in the National Education Policy 2020 (NEP
2020) for developing intellectual, aesthetic, social, physical, emotional and moral capacities of the
Chartered Accountancy students in an integrated manner. The Study Material has been developed
considering the NEP 2020 recommendations emphasizing the conceptual clarity rather than rote
learning. Emphasis is also placed on fostering the application and analytical skills by solving Multiple
Choice Questions provided at the end of each chapter. In line with the NEP 2020 recommendation
on technology-based learning, the Study Material would be supplemented by e-lectures facilitating
the experiential learning.
The syllabus of this paper on Philosophy comprises of first understanding the significance of Philosophy,
particularly from Chartered Accountancy perspective. For example, today a Chartered Accountant is
expected to work with stakeholders across different cultures and industries. Study of Philosophy will help
the Chartered Accountant in developing an understanding and further connecting the various dots of
events around the world. To help in this process, the Study Material after the introduction to Philosophy,
various branches of Philosophy (Indian and Western), introduces the reader to the significance of critical
thinking. Since Philosophy is about developing the mindset or attitude,  it requires the professional to
master the art of thinking and its applicability in various scenarios.
The student is therefore introduced to the various aspects of argumentation and evaluation. While on this
topic, the learner  is also walked thru the various fallacies one should guard against for effective
evaluation. As the learner reads further, clear parallels are drawn between the teachings of Socrates,
Plato and Aristotle and the teachings of Bhagavad Gita. As the learnings from Bhagavad Gita are
identified as the oldest and holistic, the further study of Philosophy includes a detailed study of the major
principles and teachings from Bhagavad Gita relevant to the business world. These teachings are
presented in a systematic manner with pictorial representation and examples to help the learner in the
process of acquiring sufficient ammunition to get initiated into the process of becoming a global
professional.

       PAPER-2: PSYCHOLOGY & PHILOSOPHY (100 MARKS)

              SECTION B: PHILOSOPHY (50 MARKS)

Objective

(a)    To develop an understanding of the usefulness of philosophy in contemporary life.

(b)    To develop an understanding of the philosophical methods of critical thinking and
        analytical reasoning and know who we are and how we ought to conduct ourselves.

Contents

1.    General Discussions on Philosophy and Reasoning
             i     What is Philosophy?
             ii    Core branches in Philosophy (Indian and Western)
             -     Epistemology (Theory of Knowledge- Truth, Justification and Belief)
             -     Metaphysics  (Nature  of  Reality:  Mind-Body  Dualism,  Nature  of
                 Consciousness)
             -     Axiology (Ethics and Aesthetics)
            iii     Characteristics of Indian Philosophy: Darshana Shastra
2.     Critical Thinking and Reasoning
             i     Argument and Evaluation
             ii     Informal Fallacy
3.    Ethics: Who we are & How we ought to conduct ourselves
           i.     Virtue Ethics and Theory of Excellence: Plato and Aristotle
             -     Teleology (Theory of Means and End)
             -     Deontology (Concept of Duty)
